Quick Reminder

  • Introduction: usually sets up the broad problem, gives brief context, highlights the gap, and states the purpose and research question.
  • Literature Review: usually defines concepts, reviews prior studies, introduces theoretical frameworks, and develops a deeper scholarly background.
  • Ordering: readers usually move from broader context toward a more specific focus.
